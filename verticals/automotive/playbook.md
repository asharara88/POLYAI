# Automotive — vertical playbook

Default patterns for any automotive client (OEMs, dealerships, used-car platforms, fleet sellers). Override in `clients/<slug>/knowledge/` when the client's actual situation differs.

## Audience archetypes (starting points for ICP)

- **New-car retail buyer — primary household vehicle**: practical, family-driven, balances payment and total-cost-of-ownership; shortlists 3–5 models before showroom.
- **New-car retail buyer — enthusiast / lifestyle**: brand-attached, watches reviews and launches, less price-sensitive on the chosen model.
- **Used-car buyer — value-driven**: cross-shops platforms, condition + history-report focused, decisive once shortlist forms.
- **Lease customer**: payment-anchored decision; lease-end is the strongest trigger event in the category.
- **EV-considerers**: trigger-driven (incentives, fuel prices, peer influence), high research cycle, charging-infrastructure anxiety.
- **Fleet / SMB buyer**: B2B-style, multi-vehicle, total-cost-of-ownership and service network dominate.
- **Service / aftersales customer**: existing owner, retention play more than acquisition.

## Trigger events

- **Lease expiry** — single highest-converting trigger; typically 60–120 days out
- **Warranty expiry** — service-retention trigger
- **Life events**: new child, move, job change, retirement, becoming a multi-car household
- **Macro**: fuel-price spikes, EV incentives, model-year changeover, new model launches, manufacturer recalls
- **Service moments**: a major repair quote often triggers replacement consideration

## Typical sales motion

- **Cycle**:
  - Used: 1–14 days
  - New retail: 14–60 days
  - Lease: 7–30 days from quote to delivery
  - Fleet: 60–180 days
- **Stakeholders**: spouse/partner almost always co-decides on retail; finance/HR involved on fleet
- **Critical objections**: monthly payment, residual / resale value, fuel economy or range, service network, trade-in valuation
- **Closing pattern**: research → online configurator / VDP → test drive → offer / quote → finance → delivery

## Channel mix (default weights, refine with client data)

- **Paid search** — dominant; bottom-funnel intent ("model + city", "model + lease deal") is the workhorse
- **OEM website + dealer locator + VDP** — VDP-quality is decisive for used; configurator UX for new
- **YouTube / video** — review-watching behavior is universal; OEM content + creator partnerships
- **Marketplaces** (region-specific: Cars.com, AutoTrader, Carfax, Dubizzle, etc.) — used-car table stakes
- **Meta / Instagram** — strong for launch and lifestyle; weaker for pure conversion
- **TikTok** — rising for younger buyers; format-native review content
- **CRM email + SMS** — owners-base retention, lease-end nurture, service reminders
- **Direct mail + outbound calling** — lease-end and trade-in plays still work in many markets
- **Local radio / OOH** — context-dependent; measurable only with disciplined attribution

## KPI defaults

- **Top-of-funnel**: VDP / configurator views; cost per qualified lead by source; impression share on bottom-funnel terms
- **Mid-funnel**: lead-to-test-drive rate; quote-to-test-drive rate; speed-to-lead (under 15 minutes is a real differentiator)
- **Bottom-funnel**: test-drive-to-close rate; finance attach; trade-in attach; aftersales attach
- **Retention**: service-retention rate; lease-renewal rate; owner-base reactivation rate

## Compliance flags (always check per market)

- **Financial promotion / lending disclosures** — APR, total amount payable, representative example (US: Truth in Lending; UK: FCA; EU: Consumer Credit Directive; UAE: Central Bank)
- **MPG / range / efficiency claims** — must use approved test cycle; "real-world" claims require footnotes
- **EV incentives** — eligibility small print is regulator-scrutinized in most markets
- **Lease / finance term claims** — "from $X/month" must show full disclosure on creative
- **Image rights** — model-year photography, optional-pack disclosures
- **Data privacy** — owners-base outreach consent, especially for third-party offers

## Voice notes (default — override per client)

- **Specific over evocative.** Range numbers, payment numbers, service-network counts beat "thrill of the drive" copy.
- **Honest payment math.** Hidden fees are the #1 reason buyers feel burned and post negative reviews.
- **Differentiate retail vs. fleet voice.** Same brand, different language, different proof points.
- **Watch the EV-tribalism risk.** Anti-ICE or anti-EV framing alienates a chunk of the market.

## VoC sources

- Salesperson notes (CRM activity logs)
- Service-bay feedback at retention touchpoints
- Test-drive feedback forms
- Marketplace and Google reviews (dealer level)
- Forums and YouTube comment sections (model-specific)
- NPS / post-delivery surveys

## Common pitfalls

- **Speed-to-lead failure.** Inbound leads on automotive die fast. Anything over 15 minutes loses meaningful conversion. Fix this before optimizing creative.
- **OEM ↔ dealer attribution wars.** Don't let measurement debates between OEM and dealer obscure which channels actually drive deliveries.
- **VDP / listing quality.** For used, the listing is the salesperson. Bad photos and missing fields kill more deals than weak ad copy.
- **Lease-renewal complacency.** Automakers and dealers leak retention by waiting too long; the renewal motion should start 90+ days before lease end.
- **Discount-as-marketing.** Cutting price feels like marketing and is actually finance. Tag the cost.

## Sub-vertical hints

- **OEM (manufacturer) marketing**: brand + model demand; hand off retail to dealer network with clean attribution
- **Single-rooftop dealer**: local SEO, marketplace presence, speed-to-lead — these three beat almost everything else
- **Used-car platform**: VDP quality, history-report integration, financing flow are the product
- **Fleet / leasing**: B2B sales motion, total-cost-of-ownership-led pitches, RFP responsiveness
