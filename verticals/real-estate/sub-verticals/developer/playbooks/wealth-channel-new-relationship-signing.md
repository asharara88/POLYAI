# Play — Wealth channel new relationship signing

> Owned by `wealth-channel-enablement`. Establish a new private bank, family office, or independent introducer relationship — through senior introduction, not cold approach.

## When to run it

- Coverage gap identified: geography, diaspora corridor, asset-class fit
- Quarterly channel-development KPI calls for new signings
- A new launch creates a buyer profile we don't currently reach (e.g. ultra-prime in a new market)

## Diagnose first

1. **What's the gap, specifically?** "We need more wealth-channel" is not actionable. "We have no UK-based introducer relationship reaching the Asian-British HNI corridor" is.
2. **What does a good counterparty look like?** Sub-channel (private bank vs. FO vs. introducer), AUM band, client roster fit, principal reputation, jurisdiction-rules fit.
3. **Who can introduce us?** Cold approach to a wealth-channel counterparty is a near-certain rejection. The introduction matters more than the offering.
4. **Are we ready?** Confidential pack current? Compliance flags clear (PEP / sanctions / jurisdiction)? Reciprocity capacity available?

## Target outcome

Within one quarter:
- Senior-introduction-mediated first meeting held
- Counterparty added to registry as `cultivating`
- Confidential pack delivered and acknowledged
- One mutual reciprocity touch (their event attended, our event invited)
- One introduction within the next 6 months OR clean confirmation of multi-quarter cultivation cycle

## Success criteria

- Meeting held within 60 days of introduction
- Counterparty added to registry with `cultivating` status, named principal, gatekeeper map populated
- Compliance screening clean
- Confidential pack delivered and acknowledged
- ≥1 reciprocity touch within 90 days

## Anti-patterns

- **Cold approach.** Sending a deck to a private bank's wealth-management division without an introduction does not work.
- **Generic pack.** A wealth-channel pack identical for every counterparty signals lack of thought. Tailor to their thesis.
- **Hasty commercial.** Bringing a commission grid or referral-fee proposal to the first meeting is usually wrong. Build the relationship first; commercial follows trust.
- **Skipping PEP / sanctions / jurisdiction screening.** Discovering a screening issue mid-relationship is a much worse outcome than slowing the cultivation by a week.
- **Confusing private banks with introducers.** Banks have fiduciary restrictions on introducer fees in most jurisdictions. Approaching a private bank with a fee structure is a category error.

## Variants

- **Private bank:** the relationship is institutional; we're being added to their curated-opportunity list. No fee. Their value is access to their HNI client base via banker-led referrals.
- **Multi-family office:** principal-to-principal trust is everything. Often no formal commercial; the value is mutual reciprocity over years.
- **Single-family office:** smaller, more idiosyncratic. The principal may be the buyer themselves rather than an introducer.
- **Independent introducer (IFA / RIA / wealth-tax / concierge):** clear commercial relationship; document the referral fee, ensure jurisdiction-permitted, file with legal.
