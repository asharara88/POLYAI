# Play — Broker channel reactivation

> Owned by `broker-enablement`. Bring a dormant broker firm back to producing — without giving away terms or signaling desperation.

## When to run it

- Broker firm has zero allocation requests in 90+ days (was previously active)
- Broker firm's tier-1 / tier-2 firms are dormant when the launch needs channel breadth
- Channel-development KPI for the quarter calls for ≥1 reactivation

## Diagnose first

Before any outreach, answer:

1. **Why did they stop?** Pull from CRM activity, last conversation notes, dispute log:
   - Did they leave for a competing developer's launch?
   - Did the principal broker change firms?
   - Did a dispute go unresolved? (commission, lead-overlap, allocation refusal)
   - Did our materials / pricing / payment plans become uncompetitive for their buyer pool?
   - Did they simply not get the inventory they wanted at a prior launch?
2. **Are they still viable?** Check public market signals — recent listings, public rankings, hiring, social presence. Dormant for 18+ months without market signal = de-prioritize.
3. **Who's the right person now?** The principal broker may have changed; the current decision-maker at the firm may be different. Confirm before approaching.
4. **What's the unique offer?** What can we put in front of them that they didn't have access to before?

## Target outcome

Within one quarter:
- Reactivated firm submits ≥1 allocation request
- New broker-tier confirmation signed (re-confirms commission grid + RERA pack)
- Firm attends ≥1 broker enablement event

## Sequence

| Step | Owner | Action | Approval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | broker-enablement | Pull dispute / activity history; identify root-cause hypothesis | — |
| 2 | broker-enablement | Confirm current principal contact via mutual relationship or LinkedIn check | — |
| 3 | broker-enablement | Draft a 1:1 reactivation note from Head of Sales (not bulk; named-recipient) | Head of Sales |
| 4 | Head of Sales (human) | Send the note; offer a coffee / sales-gallery walk-through | — |
| 5 | broker-enablement | Schedule the meeting; prep refreshed materials (current pricing, payment plans, available inventory) | — |
| 6 | events | If reactivation candidate is tier-1, layer in next private preview invitation | events agent |
| 7 | broker-enablement | First allocation request received → approve/process per rules; do not "make easy" — same gates apply | — |
| 8 | broker-enablement | Append reactivation outcome to `clients/<slug>/brokers/registry.md` |  knowledge agent |

## Success criteria

- Allocation request received within 60 days of meeting
- Firm marked `active` again in registry
- No special-terms discount given to win them back (would set precedent across network)

## Anti-patterns

- **Mass reactivation campaign.** Bulk emails to dormant firms read as desperate. Always 1:1.
- **Bypassing the gatekeeper.** If the firm has a head of sales / partnerships, route through them. Approaching individual brokers within the firm without going through the leader breaks the relationship.
- **Special pricing as the lure.** Reactivation via terms creates two-tier pricing across the network. Use access (preview, allocation window, credible inventory) as the lure instead.
- **Ignoring the diagnosis.** A firm that left over an unresolved commission dispute won't return until the dispute is acknowledged. Reactivate cleanly: name the issue.

## Variants

- **Tier-1 reactivation:** layer in a developer-principal-led 1:1 (CMO + Head of Sales + principal broker). Higher-touch.
- **Tier-2/3 reactivation:** can run as a small-group session with 4–6 dormant tier-2/3 firms together at the sales gallery. Group dynamic helps.
- **Cross-launch reactivation:** if firm went to a competing launch, time the reactivation around that competing launch's sustain phase (T+8 of theirs) when their attention returns.
