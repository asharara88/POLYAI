# Play — Broker channel new firm signing

> Owned by `broker-enablement`. Recruit a new broker firm to fill an identified coverage gap — geography, language, segment specialization, capacity.

## When to run it

- Quarterly channel-development KPI calls for ≥N new signings (per `client-profile.md`)
- A coverage gap is observed: e.g. "no Hindi-speaking tier-2 firm with prior diaspora-India closes"
- Network capacity below launch demand
- A high-performing broker leaves a firm; they may be recruitable solo or via a new firm

## Diagnose first

1. **What gap is being filled?** Geography, language, ICP segment, capacity, or specialization. Be specific — vague "we need more brokers" leads to weak signings.
2. **What does good look like?** A profile of the firm — size, specialization, prior conversion in the gap segment, principal-broker reputation.
3. **What can we offer them?** Most firms aren't desperate to sign — they need a reason. Possible offers: tier-1 access, exclusive-window allocation on relevant inventory, prominent placement at events, joint-marketing with the developer principal.
4. **What's our reputation in this segment?** If the developer is unknown to the target firm's principal, lead with a senior introduction; don't cold-approach.

## Target outcome

Within one quarter:
- Signed broker agreement (commission grid, RERA pack acknowledgment, firm status active)
- Firm attends mandatory training session
- Firm submits first allocation request within 30 days of signing

## Sequence

| Step | Owner | Action | Approval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | broker-enablement | Build target list of 3–5 firms that close the identified gap | — |
| 2 | broker-enablement | Score targets: principal-broker reputation, gap fit, capacity, willingness signals | — |
| 3 | broker-enablement | Identify mutual-relationship intro path for top 2 targets | — |
| 4 | Head of Sales (human) | Make the intro / first call | — |
| 5 | broker-enablement | Prepare the firm-onboarding pack: offer summary, commission grid, RERA project pack, training schedule | Head of Sales + Compliance |
| 6 | Head of Sales (human) | Onboarding meeting at sales gallery (developer principal attends if tier-1 sign) | — |
| 7 | broker-enablement | Issue commission tier confirmation; add to registry as `new` | sales-operations |
| 8 | broker-enablement | Schedule training + first preview-event invitation; track first allocation request | — |
| 9 | broker-enablement | At 30 days: review first activity; promote / confirm tier or flag concerns | — |

## Success criteria

- Signed firm submits ≥1 allocation request within 30 days
- Firm progresses to ≥3 allocation requests in first 60 days (signal of intent + execution)
- Promoted from `new` to `tier-2/3 active` in registry
- No commission disputes in first 90 days

## Anti-patterns

- **Sign-anyone-with-a-RERA-license.** A weak firm consumes onboarding resources and produces no volume; worse, it signals a desperate developer.
- **Generic offer.** "We have a new launch" is not a reason for a tier-1 firm to add another developer to their roster. Lead with the specific angle (your buyer pool, this inventory, this term).
- **Bypassing the principal broker.** Onboarding a junior broker without their principal's blessing creates internal friction at the firm.
- **Promising tier-1 access without observable performance.** Tier-1 promises early breed resentment in the existing tier-1 community. Sign tier-2 by default; promote on observed performance.

## Variants

- **Diaspora-corridor specialist:** 70% of the value is the firm's existing client roster, not their UAE presence. Signing a UK-based firm with a UAE remote arm is fine if the UK roster is dense in the right segment.
- **Solo-broker-recruitment:** when a senior individual leaves a firm, sometimes the better signing is them (with their book) at a smaller new firm or as a new firm. Tier them carefully — solo firms have capacity ceilings.
- **Multi-firm group signing:** at large launches, signing 4–6 mid-tier firms in a single onboarding wave can be more efficient than serial signings. Group event format works for this.
