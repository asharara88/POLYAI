# Real estate — vertical playbook

Default patterns for any real estate client. Override in `clients/<slug>/knowledge/` when the client's actual situation differs.

## Audience archetypes (starting points for ICP)

- **End-user buyer — primary residence**: emotional decision, longest research cycle, sensitive to school zones / commute / community signals. Looks at 30–60 listings online before contacting.
- **End-user buyer — second home / vacation**: lifestyle-driven, less price-sensitive than primary, season-sensitive in many markets.
- **Investor — yield buyer**: rational, spreadsheet-driven, cares about gross yield, vacancy, service charges, exit liquidity.
- **Investor — capital appreciation**: longer hold, watches macro indicators (rates, supply pipeline, regulatory changes).
- **Seller — life-event driven**: divorce, inheritance, relocation, downsizing. Time-sensitive, emotional.
- **Seller — opportunistic**: testing the market, will pull listing if not getting expected price.
- **Tenant turning into buyer**: lease ending, can usually be triggered 4–6 months before lease expiry.
- **Developer / institutional**: B2B-style sale, longer cycle, multi-stakeholder.

## Trigger events

- Life events: marriage, child, divorce, relocation, inheritance, retirement, lease expiry
- Macro: interest-rate moves, regulation changes, new infrastructure announcements (metro lines, schools, malls)
- Project events: new launch, handover phase, snagging period, payment plan inflection
- Seasonal: school-year cycles, summer/winter migration patterns in seasonal markets

## Typical sales motion

- **Cycle**: 30–180 days from first inquiry to close for end-users; 7–30 days for transactional investors in liquid markets.
- **Stakeholders**: usually a couple (both partners must be aligned), sometimes parents/in-laws as silent influencers, often an external mortgage broker.
- **Critical objections**: financing, school catchment, resale value, service charges, snagging history, developer reputation.
- **Closing pattern**: viewings → second viewing with influencer → offer → MOU/agreement → financing → transfer.

## Channel mix (default weights, refine with client data)

- **Property portals** (region-specific: Zillow, Realtor.com, Rightmove, Bayut, Property Finder, Magicbricks, etc.) — typically 40–60% of leads
- **Local SEO + Google Business Profile** — high-intent, low-volume, high-conversion
- **Paid search** — heavy on bottom-funnel terms ("3-bedroom + neighborhood + price")
- **Meta / Instagram** — visual-first, lifestyle-led; strong for new launches
- **YouTube / video tours** — rising channel; differentiator if competitors are still photo-only
- **WhatsApp / SMS** — high open-rate, region-dependent; treat as a 1:1 channel, not broadcast
- **Referrals** — under-invested in many agencies; structured referral programs compound
- **Email / newsletters** — works for investor lists, weak for primary-residence end-users
- **Open houses / events** — context-dependent, expensive but high-conversion

## KPI defaults

- **Top-of-funnel**: cost per lead by source; portal-listing-view-to-inquiry rate; impression share on bottom-funnel paid search
- **Mid-funnel**: lead-to-viewing rate; viewing-to-offer rate; days from inquiry to first viewing
- **Bottom-funnel**: offer-to-close rate; days on market; list-to-sold price ratio
- **Account/agent level**: listings won per agent per quarter; commission per agent

## Compliance flags (always check per market)

- Fair housing / non-discrimination rules in advertising (US: Fair Housing Act; UK: Equality Act; UAE: RERA + general consumer protection)
- Disclosure rules: agent licensing, dual-agency, broker fees, off-plan project disclosures
- Financial promotion rules when advertising payment plans / mortgage offers
- Image rights for unit photos and renderings (especially renders that don't match final delivery)
- Data privacy on lead capture (consent for WhatsApp / call follow-up)

## Voice notes (default — override per client)

- **Lean concrete.** "$1,400 service charges/sqft" beats "competitively-priced amenities."
- **Avoid superlatives without proof.** "Premium," "luxury," "exclusive" are noise unless backed by something specific.
- **Use the audience's vocabulary.** Investors don't say "yields" the same way end-users do; both differ from developers.
- **Mind the photos.** A great agent voice ruined by stock-image villas reads as fake.

## VoC sources

- Closed-deal client interviews (highest signal — what almost killed the deal?)
- Lost-deal notes from agents
- Portal reviews (Bayut, Zillow agent ratings, Google reviews)
- WhatsApp conversation logs (with consent and PII handling)
- Open-house question patterns

## Common pitfalls

- **Lead-quality complaints** are usually a routing/qualification problem, not a marketing problem. Tighten the inbound-qualifier first before turning down lead gen.
- **Listing-photo + portal-copy quality** outperforms most paid-channel optimization. Fix the listing before scaling spend.
- **Agent-level performance dispersion** is huge. Agency-wide marketing tactics fail because the bottom 50% of agents can't convert what marketing brings in. Surface this before scaling.
- **Over-promising returns** in investor-targeted copy is a regulatory and reputational landmine. Always run by `compliance`.

## Sub-vertical hints

- **Off-plan / new launch**: payment-plan clarity is the campaign. Renderings vs. delivery reality is the trust battle.
- **Luxury / branded residences**: discretion-led marketing, lower volume, higher production value, longer cycle.
- **Used / resale**: SEO + portals dominate; condition and snagging history are decisive.
- **Commercial**: B2B motion — looks more like enterprise sales than consumer; long cycle, multi-stakeholder.
