# Payment-plan structures

> **Read this first.** Every structure below is *framework reference*. The specific current escrow-law application (Dubai Law 8/2007, Abu Dhabi Law No. 3 of 2015), specific current Trustee Account disclosure requirements, specific current payment-plan-disclosure regulator language verify per request via `regulatory-research-specialist`. Bespoke structures additionally require `legal-liaison` review.

## The two regulatory anchors

Every payment-plan structure must respect two fundamentals:

1. **Escrow-protection of buyer funds** — Dubai Law 8/2007 (Dubai) / Abu Dhabi Law No. 3 of 2015 (Abu Dhabi). Buyer funds for off-plan are held in a Trustee Account at a Trustee Bank; release tied to construction milestones.
2. **Disclosure** — buyer must be informed of payment plan + escrow mechanics + risk allocation per `.claude/skills/regulatory-disclosure-language.md` Template 3.

A payment plan that violates either anchor is non-compliant regardless of commercial appeal.

## Standard structures

### Structure A — During-construction balanced (60/40 typical)

| Stage | % of price | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reservation | 5-10% | At reservation form signing | To Trustee Account |
| Construction milestones | 50-55% | Per construction-progress milestones (foundation, structure, façade, MEP, interiors) | Per regulator-recognized milestone schedule |
| **Subtotal during construction** | **60%** | | |
| Handover | 40% | At handover (or per 30/30 split: 30% pre-handover + 30% on title transfer in some variants) | |

**Pros:** Predictable cash-flow for developer; aligns with construction spend; standard disclosure language.
**Cons:** High during-construction commitment for buyers; sensitive to construction-progress trust.

### Structure B — Handover-heavy (40/60)

| Stage | % of price | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Reservation | 5-10% | At reservation |
| Construction milestones | 30-35% | Per milestones |
| **Subtotal during construction** | **40%** | |
| Handover | 60% | At handover |

**Pros:** Lower during-construction commitment; attractive to buyers wanting to defer commitment.
**Cons:** Heavier handover concentration; cash-flow strain on developer mid-construction; escrow-coverage adequacy must be confirmed.

### Structure C — Post-handover plan (60/40 + post-handover schedule)

| Stage | % of price | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Reservation | 5-10% | At reservation |
| Construction | 50-55% | Per milestones |
| Handover | 20-25% | At handover |
| Post-handover | 15-20% | Over 1-3 years post-handover (typical 6-month or annual installments) |

**Pros:** Lower handover lump-sum; attractive to buyers managing liquidity post-handover; signals developer confidence in post-handover relationship.
**Cons:** Capital tied up post-handover; collection risk; post-handover late-payment escalation framework needed.

### Structure D — 40/40/20 (mortgage-friendly)

| Stage | % of price | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| Reservation + early construction | 40% | Reservation + first milestones |
| Late construction + handover | 40% | Late milestones + handover |
| Mortgage drawdown | 20% | Buyer's mortgage facility drawdown post-handover |

**Pros:** Aligns with typical CBUAE mortgage LTV caps for non-residents (verify current via `regulatory-research-specialist`); attractive to buyers using mortgage finance.
**Cons:** Mortgage drawdown timing risk; coordination with banks required; CBUAE LTV regime governs.

## Bespoke structures (route through `deal-desk-analyst`)

### Structure E — Deferred deposit

Reservation deposit reduced or zero; first major payment at first construction milestone. Used selectively for strategic-value buyers; `deal-desk-analyst` analyzes margin-impact and precedent-setting risk.

### Structure F — Capital-recycling (multi-unit / floor / building)

For buyers acquiring multiple units / a floor / a building: payment cadence aligned with the buyer's own cash-recycling (e.g., revenue from earlier units funds later units). Highly bespoke; `legal-liaison` redlines; senior-management approval.

### Structure G — Asset-swap

Buyer contributes existing real-estate or other asset toward purchase price. Bespoke; legal valuation + AML/KYC on the contributed asset; `legal-liaison` + tax counsel typically engaged.

### Structure H — Capitalized-rent

For a unit the buyer intends to lease: a portion of the price is satisfied via capitalized future rent. Highly bespoke; investment-product framing risk per `compliance`; `legal-liaison` + `regulatory-research-specialist` review for SEC / private-placement / FCA / similar implications by buyer-corridor.

### Structure I — Bespoke for VVIP / strategic counterparty

Custom structure per principal — combination of standard structures + concessions + bespoke timing. Always restricted-access per `.claude/skills/vvip-protocol-uae.md`.

## Cash-flow + IRR implications (developer-side)

| Structure | Developer cash-flow shape | IRR implication |
|---|---|---|
| A. 60/40 balanced | Steady during construction; large handover lump | Standard baseline |
| B. 40/60 handover-heavy | Lighter during construction; very large handover | Lower IRR (cash-flow timing); higher escrow-coverage requirement |
| C. Post-handover 60/40+pH | Steady during construction; lighter handover; trail post-handover | Lower IRR; collection risk; post-handover capital lockup |
| D. 40/40/20 mortgage-friendly | Standard during construction; mortgage drawdown timing | Coordination cost; standard IRR if drawdown on time |
| E. Deferred deposit | Light at start; heavier at first milestone | Margin-impact tradeoff vs. strategic value |
| F. Capital-recycling | Custom per deal | Per case |
| G. Asset-swap | Lump at swap completion | Per asset valuation; tax implications |
| H. Capitalized-rent | Up-front discount + rental-income tail | Complex IRR; investment-product framing risk |
| I. Bespoke VVIP | Per case | Per case |

`marketing-financial-manager` runs cash-flow modeling per `deal-desk-analyst` analysis.

## Disclosure language

Every payment plan in customer-facing material carries disclosure per `.claude/skills/regulatory-disclosure-language.md` Template 3:

- Plan structure stated
- Trustee Account reference
- Applicable escrow law referenced
- Trustee Bank named
- Buyer-protection-on-non-completion language

Bespoke structures additionally carry:

- Bespoke disclosure-language additions per `legal-liaison` review
- Where investment-product framing is present, financial-promotion overlays per Template 2

## Forbidden in payment-plan customer-facing materials

- Implied risk-free language (per Template 2 forbidden list)
- Implied guaranteed completion ("project will complete by X" without forward-looking caveat per Template 7)
- Implied guaranteed mortgage approval (mortgage subject to CBUAE rules + lender's own terms)
- Implied guaranteed visa eligibility on Golden Visa pathway (per Template 5)
- Misleading present-tense framing on future events

## Mortgage-overlay framework

Where buyer intends to use mortgage finance:

- **CBUAE Mortgage Regulation 31/2013 + amendments** — LTV caps for residents and non-residents differ by buyer category, property value, primary vs. additional property; verify current via `regulatory-research-specialist` per case
- **Pre-approval recommended** — buyer engages with bank pre-reservation to confirm financing capacity
- **Drawdown timing** — typically post-handover; some banks support late-construction drawdown
- **Buyer-corridor implications** — international buyers using cross-border mortgage finance face their home-jurisdiction rules + UAE rules
- **AML/KYC continuity** — mortgage lender does its own KYC; developer-side KYC continues per DNFBP

## Post-handover collection framework (Structure C + variants)

For post-handover payment plans:

- Collection cadence published in SPA + reservation form
- Late-payment grace period typical (30-60 days)
- Late-payment fee structure per SPA + applicable consumer-protection rules
- Escalation pathway: `account-manager` (relationship) → `marketing-financial-manager` (collection coordination) → `legal-liaison` (formal demand) → courts / arbitration per SPA dispute clause
- Title-deed handover may be conditional on full payment in some jurisdictions (verify per case)

## Escrow-coverage adequacy check

For Structure B (40/60 handover-heavy) and any structure with low during-construction collection:

- Trustee Bank confirms escrow coverage adequacy at agreement
- Construction-cost coverage from milestone payments must remain ≥ developer's working-capital floor
- Material adverse change in market (lower-than-expected sales rate) triggers escrow-coverage review
- `marketing-financial-manager` monitors continuously
