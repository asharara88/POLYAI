# Creative brief — Saadiyat Reserve Heights — Hero landing page

> **Worked example — illustrative.** Not real UAE Developments data.
>
> Owned by `strategy`. Conforms to `schemas/creative-brief.md`.
> Will be consumed by `creative` (copy) + `brand-design` (visuals) + `seo` (technical SEO + on-page).
> Stamped with `client: uae-developments, vertical: real-estate, campaign: q3-tower-launch`.

```yaml
asset_id: q3-tower-launch__hero-landing
parent_campaign_id: q3-tower-launch
asset_type: landing-page
audience: Segment A — GCC investor (yield + appreciation). Segment B served by a /uk and /in localized variant produced by localization.
single_minded_message: |
  This is a residence engineered for a generation, with the financial transparency of a private bank,
  operated from day one by [named hospitality partner].
emotional_response: Quiet confidence. "These people respect my time and my intelligence."
rational_takeaway: Specific architect + specific operator + specific service charge + RERA-compliant escrow + named rental-pool program. No vague promises.
call_to_action: Request the investor pack
proof_points:
  - Architectural firm: [named firm] (the audience knows their previous work)
  - Hospitality operator: [named operator] (the audience has stayed at their hotels)
  - Service charge: AED 1,540/sqft per annum, capped for first 5 years post-handover
  - Payment plan: regulator-compliant escrow-protected (ADGM / RERA as applicable), 60/40 structure across handover
  - Rental pool: managed by hospitality operator, projected 5–7% net yield (not guaranteed)
mandatories:
  - Regulator registration number visible (ADGM / RERA as applicable)
  - Off-plan disclosure paragraph above the fold (compliance-supplied wording)
  - Service-charge figure visible without scrolling on desktop
  - Construction status as of this month (third-party-verified link)
constraints:
  word_count: ≤ 90 words above the fold; total page ≤ 600 words
  format: responsive landing page; AR variant produced by localization
  brand_voice_ref: clients/_examples/uae-developments/knowledge/brand-voice.md
  do:
    - Lead with a specific number or named partner, not an adjective
    - Use the buyer's portfolio language ("yield + appreciation profile")
    - Show payment-plan structure on the page, not behind a click
  dont:
    - "Luxury," "premium," "exclusive," "elite," "iconic," "world-class"
    - Any "guaranteed" anything
    - Generic skyline imagery; no helicopter; no champagne
    - "Limited-time" pressure language
references:
  good:
    - Aman residences microsites (restraint, white space, named-partner trust)
    - Private bank investor pages (data-density done with restraint)
  bad:
    - Generic UAE off-plan landing pages with helicopter shots and "iconic landmark" hero copy
deliverables:
  - English desktop + mobile
  - Arabic desktop + mobile (handed to localization)
  - UK variant /uk (currency display + diaspora trust signals)
  - India variant /in (diaspora trust signals; English copy retained)
deadline: 2026-05-30 (in time for invitation-only window)
review_path:
  1. brand-design: visual on-brand check
  2. seo: on-page + schema + tracking
  3. compliance: ADGM + RERA + financial-promotion + GDPR (mandatory)
  4. review: final QA against this brief
  5. chief-commercial-officer → human approval before publish
```

## Notes for `creative` (the agent that will write the copy)

- The page is for someone who has bought property before. Don't define off-plan. Don't explain "what is a service charge."
- Do not mention competitors by name; do not compare yields directly.
- Variant A (yield-led) and Variant B (architecture-led) hero headlines are the A/B test specified in the campaign brief — produce both; do not collapse to one.
- The CTA is not "Buy now." It is "Request the investor pack." This is a content-asset gate, not a sales close.
