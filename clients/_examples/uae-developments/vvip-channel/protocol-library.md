## Forms of address — quick reference

### UAE / GCC ruling families

- **Ruler of an Emirate / Crown Prince:**
  - Full first reference: "His Highness Sheikh [first name] bin [father's name] Al [family name], [title]"
  - Subsequent: "His Highness" or "Sheikh [first name]"
  - Written abbreviation: "H.H. Sheikh ..."
- **Other ruling-family members:** "His Highness Sheikh [first name]" / "Her Highness Sheikha [first name]"
- **KSA royal family — King:** "His Majesty King [name]" / "His Royal Majesty"
- **KSA royal family — Crown Prince:** "His Royal Highness Crown Prince [name]" / "HRH"
- **KSA royal family — other princes / princesses:** "His Royal Highness Prince [name]" / "Her Royal Highness Princess [name]"
- **Kuwait — Emir:** "His Highness the Amir, Sheikh [name]"
- **Qatar — Emir:** "His Highness the Amir, Sheikh [name]"

### Government — UAE federal and emirate level

- **Federal cabinet ministers:** "His Excellency [first name] [last name]" / "Her Excellency [first name] [last name]"
  - Written: "H.E. [name]"
- **Ministers of State:** same as above
- **Undersecretaries / Director-General level:** "His Excellency" / "Her Excellency"
- **Ambassadors (UAE):** "His Excellency Ambassador [name]"

### Government — UK

- **Cabinet ministers:** "The Right Honourable [first name] [last name] MP"
- **Other ministers / MPs:** "The Honourable [name] MP" or just "[name] MP"
- **Peers:** "The Right Honourable Lord / Lady [name]" or per specific peerage
- **Knighthood / Damehood:** "Sir [first name]" / "Dame [first name]"

### Government — India

- **Union Cabinet Minister / Minister of State:** "Her Excellency [name]" / "His Excellency [name]" in international protocol; "The Honourable" in some forums
- **Ambassadors:** "His / Her Excellency Ambassador [name]"

### Foreign diplomatic

- **Heads of state:** local convention; verify before drafting (His / Her Majesty for monarchs; His / Her Excellency for elected; "Mr / Madam President" some protocols)
- **Ambassadors generally:** "His / Her Excellency Ambassador [name]"
- **Consuls-General:** "[name], Consul-General"

### Senior judiciary, military, sovereign-institution

- Local protocol office advises. Never assume. Default in writing: "[civilian title] [last name]" with confirmation requested.

## Ceremonial precedence — common UAE event ordering

When inviting and seating multiple VVIPs at a single event, ordering matters. The standard hierarchy in a UAE setting is approximately:

1. Ruling-family principals (by household seniority and household rank)
2. Federal cabinet ministers (by portfolio order)
3. Emirate-level cabinet (by emirate order, by portfolio)
4. Senior judiciary
5. Senior military (when in civilian setting)
6. Foreign ambassadors (by date of credential presentation, not alphabetical)
7. Visiting heads of state (when applicable, often top of the list — depends on protocol)

In practice, the protocol office of the most-senior expected attendee advises on final ordering. We do not unilaterally rank.

## Hosting norms

### UAE / GCC settings

- **Coffee + dates** offered on arrival — refusing is impolite.
- **Right hand** for shaking and receiving (when handshake is offered).
- **Ramadan:** Daytime food / drink offered to non-fasting guests only; respect fasting hosts; avoid scheduling key meetings during the last hour before iftar.
- **Eid Al Fitr / Eid Al Adha:** quiet period; no commercial outreach for several days.
- **National Day (UAE Dec 2, KSA Sept 23, etc.):** congratulatory messaging via gatekeeper appropriate.
- **Gendered protocol:** confirm in advance via gatekeeper. Default: handshake offered only if extended; if mixed-gender event, seating advised by protocol office.

### Western diplomatic settings

- Western diplomatic norms apply (rank-ordered seating, formal address through the meeting, dress code per invitation).

### Cross-cultural settings

- Default to the more conservative protocol when uncertain.

## Forms of written communication

- **Initial outreach:** never an email. Always a formal letter on developer letterhead, signed by the developer's principal, hand-delivered or couriered through the gatekeeper.
- **Follow-up:** by the gatekeeper's preferred channel (often email after the formal letter has been received and acknowledged).
- **Invitation-to-event:** formal letter or printed invitation card; include a personal note from the principal where relationship warrants.
- **Confirmation of arrangements:** through the gatekeeper, not directly to the principal.
- **Bilingual where appropriate:** Arabic primary for GCC counterparties, with English provided alongside.

## When protocol fails

- One protocol error is a problem, not a disaster. Acknowledge in writing through the gatekeeper, in the principal's name, with no defensiveness. Correct course immediately.
- Two errors with the same counterparty is the relationship ending.
- Public exposure of a protocol error (press, social) is escalated to the developer's principal + legal + comms team within an hour.
