## What worked

- **Construction-progress reveal landed strongly.** Showing the third-party-verified drone footage of Saadiyat Coastal I structural progress alongside Saadiyat Reserve Heights architectural reveal built credibility for the upcoming launch in the audience that's already familiar with the brand's delivery.
- **Hospitality operator presence.** Their participation reinforced the institutional partnership story — owners noted it explicitly in feedback.
- **Q&A run long.** Going 45 minutes instead of the planned 30 was the right call; ended the formal program with everyone in a warm posture.
- **Model-unit walk-through immediately after Q&A.** No transition gap; momentum carried into 1:1 RM conversations.
- **Catering delivery.** Fauchon's allergen-tracking and halal handling held up cleanly across 14 dietary flags.
- **No-show rate beat benchmark.** 5.2% vs. average 12% for owner events — credit to the personal RM-led RSVP nudges.

## What didn't

- **Save-the-date timing.** 6 weeks out was too early; some owners didn't keep the date in mind. Attribution: 9 of the 25 Saadiyat Coastal I "no" responses cited "calendar conflict" that probably didn't exist 6 weeks prior.
- **Arabic-language program coverage.** 8 Arabic-only attendees received the formal program in EN-primary with simultaneous translation on individual headsets. Headsets distributed late; 2 attendees missed the first 5 minutes of the construction reveal.
- **Q&A microphone discipline.** Open Q&A meant some owners dominated airtime; quieter owners with substantive questions didn't get to ask. Floor management improvement needed for next event.
- **Photography release confusion.** 3 attendees were photographed who later requested removal from any internal-distribution use. Release form was not collected at registration; should be standard.
- **Tier-1 broker overflow.** 4 tier-1 brokers asked to attend "to support their owner clients" — they're not owners themselves and weren't invited. Awkward decline at the door for 2; the other 2 attended at the developer principal's discretion. Need a clearer policy for next event.

## What we'd change

1. **Save-the-date 4 weeks out, not 6.** Same RSVP nudge cadence after that.
2. **Arabic-primary program for Arabic-confirmed attendees.** Not headsets-as-afterthought. Pre-confirmed cohort gets a separate intro session in Arabic, then joins the main program for the architectural reveal.
3. **Moderated Q&A with floor cards.** Attendees write questions on cards; events lead reads them in a balanced order. Improves coverage and signals thoughtful curation.
4. **Photography release at registration.** Add a single line to the registration confirmation: "Photographs may be taken; opt out at the door if preferred." Collect opt-outs at check-in.
5. **Broker policy at owner events.** Default-no, but allow a "1 per owner" rule when explicitly requested by the owner. Communicate the policy in the formal invitation.

## Lessons promoted to knowledge

- For owner-loyalty events: 4-week save-the-date default; bilingual primary programs (not headsets-as-afterthought) when ≥10% of confirmed attendees are non-default-language; floor-card moderated Q&A; photography release at registration.
- For Q3-launch sustain feedback loop: existing-owner cohort produced 14 reservations in 90 days (12% conversion of attendees) — strong cohort, recommend earlier and longer-runway warm-up for any future tower launch.
