# Brand voice — Aldar Developments

> **Worked example — illustrative.** Voice principles for a developer-class engagement; not real Aldar Properties brand-voice guidelines.
>
> Seeded by `client-onboarding`. Three voice words: **institutional, restrained, sovereign-affiliated**. Visual notes pulled from `verticals/real-estate/sub-verticals/developer/playbook.md` "Voice notes" + branded-residences pattern.

## Voice in one paragraph

The voice of an Abu Dhabi-anchored developer with national scale and institutional presence. It speaks with the calm of a steward of long-term assets — patient, master-planner posture, never transactional in tone. Numbers before adjectives. Specifics before promises. Implies endurance ("we plan in decades") rather than urgency. Closer in register to a sovereign wealth fund's communications than to a private branding agency's; quieter than Dubai's louder developers.

## Three voice principles

1. **Institutional patience.** "We plan in decades" framing. No urgency-led copy, no countdown clocks. Time horizon implied is generational.
2. **Specifics over superlatives.** "AED 1,540/sqft service charges, capped 5 years post-handover" beats "competitively-priced amenities." Drop "luxury," "premium," "exclusive" unless backed by something concrete (a named partner, a verified specification).
3. **Sovereign-affiliated restraint.** Aldar's brand register sits adjacent to UAE national institutions. Keep gravitas; avoid hype. The buyer is treated as already qualified — no first-time-buyer explanations, no over-explanation of off-plan basics.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with a number, a fact, or a specific named feature | Lead with adjectives or emotive flourishes |
| Reference master-community context (Saadiyat / Yas / Al Raha) by name | Use "iconic," "landmark," "world-class" |
| Show payment-plan structure transparently | Hide fees or rely on "from AED X" without disclosure |
| Speak to the investor's portfolio logic, the end-user's family logic | Speak as if this is their first purchase |
| Imply long horizons and institutional credibility | Imply scarcity-by-pressure or auction dynamics |

## Vocabulary

**Use**
- "residence" (not "unit" in customer-facing copy)
- "owners" (not "buyers" once reserved)
- "service charge" stated as AED/sqft per annum
- "handover" (not "completion" or "delivery")
- "master community" (Saadiyat, Yas, Al Raha, Al Ghadeer named explicitly when relevant)
- specific named partners and materials

**Avoid**
- "luxury," "premium," "exclusive," "elite" without context
- "guaranteed" anything (regulatory risk; ADGM + RERA flags)
- "iconic," "landmark," "world-class"
- "limited time" / urgency-pressure language
- generic "Dubai" when the project is Abu Dhabi (geography matters to the audience)

**Reframe**
- "Investment opportunity" → "yield + appreciation profile, in detail below"
- "Prime location" → name the specific district + named landmarks within walking distance
- "High-quality finishes" → name the supplier / specification

## Per-platform overrides

- **LinkedIn / B2B email**: longer-form; investor-thesis framing; named-author voice from leadership; appropriate to share institutional credentials
- **Instagram / consumer**: imagery-led, minimal copy; captions are evidence captions ("Floor 47. East-facing. AED 1,540/sqft.") rather than mood copy
- **WhatsApp**: 1:1 only, never broadcast; short, factual, named-broker voice
- **Landing pages**: progressive disclosure — headline + one fact + the next-step CTA, with full data behind a click

## Visual notes

- **Color system**: muted neutrals; a single restrained accent; no high-saturation hero shots
- **Typography**: serif for headlines (editorial register), clean sans for body; generous line-height
- **Imagery**: architectural photography over lifestyle photography; real materials over renderings where possible; honest light (golden hour acceptable, never midday hard-light filters); avoid stock-villa imagery
- **Avoid**: floating people in front of skylines, champagne glasses, helicopters, "lifestyle" stock, over-rendered marketing CGI that won't match handover
