# Brand voice — Meridian Residences

> Seeded by `client-onboarding` from intake. Three voice words from the human user: **precise, restrained, confident**.
> Visual notes pulled from intake + `verticals/real-estate/playbook.md` "Voice notes" + sub-vertical "Luxury / branded residences" pattern.

## Voice in one paragraph

Meridian sounds like a senior advisor who respects the buyer's intelligence. Numbers before adjectives. Specifics before promises. Never strives to impress; assumes the buyer already knows quality when they see it. Quiet authority — the way private banking communicates, not the way mass-market real estate does.

## Three voice principles

1. **Specifics over superlatives.** "AED 1,400/sqft service charges" beats "competitively-priced amenities." Drop "luxury," "premium," "exclusive" unless backed by something concrete.
2. **Restraint signals confidence.** Don't oversell. Short sentences. White space. The product carries the weight; the copy gets out of the way.
3. **The buyer is already qualified.** Don't explain off-plan basics in headline copy. Talk to someone who's bought before.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with a number, a fact, or a specific named feature | Lead with adjectives or emotive flourishes |
| Reference the architectural team, materials, or service operator by name | Use "world-class," "iconic," "unparalleled" |
| Show payment plan structure transparently | Hide fees or rely on "from AED X" without disclosure |
| Speak to the investor's portfolio logic | Speak as if this is their first purchase |

## Vocabulary

**Use**
- "residence" (not "unit" in customer-facing copy)
- "owners" (not "buyers" once reserved)
- "service charge" stated as AED/sqft per annum
- "handover" (not "completion" or "delivery")
- specific named partners and materials

**Avoid**
- "luxury," "premium," "exclusive," "elite" without context
- "guaranteed" anything (regulatory risk; see compliance flags)
- "iconic," "landmark," "world-class"
- "limited time" / urgency-pressure language

**Reframe**
- "Investment opportunity" → "yield + appreciation profile, in detail below"
- "Prime location" → name the specific district + the named landmarks within walking distance
- "High-quality finishes" → name the supplier / specification

## Per-platform overrides

- **LinkedIn / B2B email**: longer-form; investor-thesis framing; named-author voice from leadership
- **Instagram / consumer**: imagery-led, minimal copy, captions are evidence captions (e.g. "Floor 47. East-facing. AED 1,540/sqft." rather than mood copy)
- **WhatsApp**: 1:1 only, never broadcast; short, factual, named-broker voice
- **Landing pages**: progressive disclosure — headline + one fact + the next-step CTA, with full data behind a click

## Visual notes

- **Color system**: muted neutrals; a single restrained accent (TBD by `brand-design`); no high-saturation hero shots
- **Typography**: serif for headlines (editorial register), clean sans for body; generous line-height
- **Imagery**: architectural photography over lifestyle photography; real materials over renderings where possible; honest light (golden hour acceptable, never midday hard-light filters); avoid stock-villa imagery
- **Avoid**: floating people in front of skylines; champagne glasses; helicopters; "lifestyle" stock; over-rendered marketing CGI that won't match handover
